Mount Pleasant Neighborhoods — Full Coverage

Old Village

Historic waterfront · Pitt Street · Cooper River

Mount Pleasant's original settlement — historic homes, live oaks, and the iconic Pitt Street Bridge. Less than a mile from the Ravenel Bridge with authentic Lowcountry character.

Park West & Carolina Park

Northern MP · Rifle Range Rd / Long Point Rd

Master-planned communities housing ~30,000 residents — pools, parks, and family amenities representing Mount Pleasant's explosive 2000–2020 growth era.

I'On Village

Between Mathis Ferry Rd & Hobcaw Dr

New urbanist neighborhood with front porches, walkable squares, and traditional design — just north of the Ravenel Bridge approach and one of MP's most distinctive addresses.

Belle Hall & Dunes West

Central MP · US-17 corridor

Belle Hall's commercial hub anchors Mount Pleasant's retail and dining scene. Dunes West adds an upscale golf course community rounding out the central corridor.

Shem Creek Area

Coleman Boulevard · Intracoastal Waterway

Mount Pleasant's most famous corridor — waterfront restaurants (Red's Ice House, Tavern & Table, Water's Edge), working shrimp boats, and Charleston's best happy hour strip. Perfect for pre-party dinner before a private night in.

Waterfront & Hobcaw

Eastern MP · Wando River access

Upscale waterfront properties with Wando River and harbor views — ideal for private events with scenery to match. Direct I-526 access for Daniel Island and North Charleston bookings.

Access Routes — The Ravenel Advantage

Arthur Ravenel Jr. Bridge (US-17)

The 13,200-foot cable-stayed span across the Cooper River — 8 lanes, 186-ft clearance, and a 3–4 minute crossing to downtown Charleston's East Bay Street. Rarely experiences the backups common on James Island or West Ashley bridges.

Primary route to all downtown Charleston bookings

Isle of Palms Connector (SC-517)

8.5-mile limited-access highway from Hungryneck Boulevard directly to Palm Boulevard on IOP — high-rise Intracoastal crossing included. 12–15 minutes from US-17 to Front Beach with no residential street navigation required.

Direct fast access to IOP vacation rentals

Ben Sawyer Boulevard

Coleman Boulevard west to Sullivan's Island via the Ben Sawyer swing bridge. Slower than the IOP Connector but serves Sullivan's Island directly and provides an alternate beach route when needed.

Sullivan's Island access & IOP backup route

I-526 (Mark Clark Expressway)

The interstate bypass connecting Mount Pleasant with North Charleston, Charleston International Airport, and all of western Charleston County. Eastern terminus at US-17 near the Wando River.

Daniel Island, North Charleston & airport hotel access
